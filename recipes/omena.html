WHAT IS OMENA?

Omena (Rastrineobola argentea) is a small fresh-water fish that resembles sardines. The name omena is actually derived from the Luo language and simply refers to the silver cyprinid fish, in English. It is a popular source of food in Kenya, Tanzania, as well as other parts of Africa, where it goes by several names such as Lake Victoria sardine, Dagaa (Tanzania), or Mukene (Uganda).

It is normally prepared into a stew, shallow-fried, or deep-fried.
