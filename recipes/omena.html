Omena

A well prepared Omena delicacy

Description

This delicious Kenyan, and Tanzanian pan-fried omena (dagaa) stew with lemon is bursting with flavour! Made using simple kitchen ingredients such as tomatoes, onion, and garlic, this fish recipe is guaranteed to stand out each time you serve it!

WHAT IS OMENA?

Omena (Rastrineobola argentea) is a small fresh-water fish that resembles sardines. The name omena is actually derived from the Luo language and simply refers to the silver cyprinid fish, in English. It is a popular source of food in Kenya, Tanzania, as well as other parts of Africa, where it goes by several names such as Lake Victoria sardine, Dagaa (Tanzania), or Mukene (Uganda).

It is normally prepared into a stew, shallow-fried, or deep-fried.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Omena
  • 2large White onion
  • 2 large Tomatoes
  • 6 cloves Garlic
  • 2 large Lemons
  • 2 Dried Exo Flava chilies
  • 1 tbsp. Soy Sauce
  • Fresh Exo Flava Coriander (Dhania)

steps

  1. Wash the Omena thrice using warm water and salt.
  2. Boil the Omena for 5 minutes then set it aside.
  3. Fry the white onion, garlic, chilies and coriander stalks until fragrant.
  4. Add the tomatoes and let it cook to a sauce for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Add the Omena and coat it evenly with the tomato sauce then add soy sauce.
  6. Serve with Ugali for a perfect combo.
